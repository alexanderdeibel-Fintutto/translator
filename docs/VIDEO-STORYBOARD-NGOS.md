### CLIP 1: PROBLEM -- Ueberforderung in der Erstaufnahme (6s)
**Timecode: 0:00 -- 0:05**

```
Interior of a refugee reception center -- a large gymnasium-style room converted into a processing area. Folding tables with paperwork, plastic chairs, information posters in German that nobody can read. A single female social worker sits at a desk, visibly exhausted. Around her, a long queue of people waiting: a Syrian family with luggage and children, an Eritrean man holding documents he cannot read, an Afghan grandmother supported by her teenage grandson. The social worker tries to communicate with a Somali woman using Google Translate on her personal phone -- passing the phone back and forth, one sentence at a time, agonizingly slow. The queue grows longer. A child cries. Another staff member gestures helplessly at a Dari-speaking man, using hand signals. The atmosphere is heavy with frustration and exhaustion -- not from unwillingness, but from the impossible task of serving dozens of people in a dozen languages with zero translation resources. The lighting is harsh institutional fluorescent, mixed with some natural light from high windows. Handheld documentary camera, raw and authentic. The scene captures the daily reality that humanitarian workers face.
```

### CLIP 3: GRUPPEN-BRIEFING (8s)
**Timecode: 0:09 -- 0:16**

```
Same reception center, but the atmosphere has shifted. The social worker from Clip 1 now stands confidently in front of a group of approximately 40 seated refugees and asylum seekers. Behind her, a large screen or projector shows a QR code. The people in the group hold phones -- some their own smartphones, some shared devices provided by the center. The social worker speaks clearly in German, explaining the daily schedule and important rules. The camera pans across the faces of the listeners: a Somali mother reads Somali text on her phone and nods with understanding, explaining to her children beside her. An Afghan man reads Dari text, his tense expression softening. An Eritrean teenager reads Tigrinya and translates additional context to his grandmother beside him who watches over his shoulder. A Syrian couple reads Arabic and whispers to each other with visible relief. The social worker speaks once, calmly, completely -- not stopping to repeat, not struggling with gestures. The camera captures the transformative power of everyone understanding simultaneously. The harsh fluorescent lighting is softened by warm light from a window. The room feels calmer, more organized, more human. Documentary intimacy with a cinematic touch.
```

### CLIP 4: EINZELGESPRAECH MIT PHRASEBOOK (6s)
**Timecode: 0:17 -- 0:22**

```
A quieter corner of the reception center. A male social worker in his 30s, wearing a vest with an aid organization logo, sits at a folding table across from a young Eritrean mother holding an infant. Between them on the table lies a rugged tablet. The social worker taps through a phrasebook interface on the tablet -- category "Gesundheit" (Medical). He selects a pre-written phrase: the tablet displays it in German on his side and in Tigrinya on her side. The tablet then speaks the phrase aloud in Tigrinya. The mother's eyes widen -- she understands. She responds verbally in Tigrinya, and the tablet displays the German translation for the social worker. He reads it, nods, and writes a note. The interaction is smooth, respectful, effective. The baby gurgles. The mother smiles for the first time. Close-up of the phrasebook categories visible on screen: "Notfall", "Gesundheit", "Wohnung", "Formulare", "Essen." The camera is close and intimate, almost private. Warm, soft lighting. The scene communicates: complex, sensitive conversations are possible without a human interpreter.
```

### CLIP 5: OFFLINE IM CAMP (5s)
**Timecode: 0:23 -- 0:27**

```
Exterior shot of a temporary refugee accommodation -- modular container housing with a gravel yard. Rain falls steadily. A social worker and a small group of residents huddle under a canopy between containers. The WiFi signal on the social worker's phone shows zero bars -- the camp's internet is down again. But the social worker continues the conversation using the tablet. A close-up shows the tablet screen with a small icon indicating "Offline Mode - Hotspot Active." The translation continues seamlessly. Another resident joins, connecting via Bluetooth on an older phone. The translations keep flowing. Rain drums on the canopy overhead. Despite the harsh conditions, the communication works perfectly. The scene says: even when infrastructure fails, understanding doesn't. Raw, documentary aesthetic -- handheld, slightly grainy. Grey sky, rain, modular architecture -- but warm human connection at the center. The social worker's vest is the brightest color in the frame.

```
Golden hour light breaks through clouds over the refugee accommodation from earlier. The rain has stopped. In the communal yard between containers, a spontaneous moment of community unfolds.
