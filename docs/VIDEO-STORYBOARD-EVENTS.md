### CLIP 1: PROBLEM -- Dolmetscherkabinen (5s)
**Timecode: 0:00 -- 0:04**

```
Wide interior shot of a large international conference hall. On the main stage, a speaker presents at a podium with a large LED screen behind them. The camera then pans to the back of the hall, revealing a row of glass-walled interpreter booths -- six booths, each with two interpreters wearing headsets, speaking rapidly. Technical staff adjusts audio equipment between the booths. Thick cables run across the floor, covered by yellow safety tape. A technician kneels fixing a receiver unit. In the audience, attendees fumble with clunky receiver devices and wired earpieces -- some earpieces dangling unused, some people holding receivers to their ears looking uncomfortable. A frustrated attendee raises his hand -- his receiver doesn't work. Another woman looks at her receiver showing "Channel 3: French" but she needs Mandarin, which isn't available. The scene conveys massive infrastructure, massive cost, limited languages. Cool blue stage lighting contrasts with warm audience light. The camera movement is slow and observational, documentary-style. The interpreter booths are prominently featured as the expensive, complex status quo.
```
