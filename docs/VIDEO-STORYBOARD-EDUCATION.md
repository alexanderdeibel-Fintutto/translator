### CLIP 1: PROBLEM -- Stille Willkommensklasse (6s)
**Timecode: 0:00 -- 0:05**

```
Interior of a bright German classroom. A female teacher in her 30s stands at a whiteboard, writing a math problem. She turns to face her class of 18 students -- children aged 10-14 from diverse backgrounds: Syrian, Afghan, Eritrean, Ukrainian, Somali, Turkish. The teacher speaks in German, explaining the math problem with enthusiasm. But the class is silent. Most children stare blankly. A Syrian girl looks down at her desk, disconnected. An Afghan boy copies the numbers from the board without understanding what they mean. A Somali teenager looks out the window. An older Ukrainian girl tries to translate for a younger Eritrean boy next to her, but she barely speaks German herself. The teacher's enthusiasm fades -- she looks at her class and realizes: they can't follow. She pauses, sighs quietly, and tries again with simpler words and hand gestures. The disconnect is heartbreaking -- a dedicated teacher, eager children, but a language wall between them. Warm classroom light from large windows, colorful wall decorations, a welcoming space where learning should happen but can't. Handheld, intimate, documentary-style.
```
